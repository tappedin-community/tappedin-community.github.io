5.0 Private, Public, and Tenant Groups

5.1 Private vs. Public

A private group means that only members of the group can see that the group exists, and that to become a member, the person must be invited by another member. Private groups are not listed in the Group Directory to nonmembers. Users initially access the private group using their Unique Group ID and password;Initiators of a private group send an invitation through the system that includes the group ID and password to potential members. Once they log in, they have indicated that they wish to be a member of this group. The group will automatically appear in their lists of groups on their Me tab, subtab Groups.The member can then access and remove themselves from the group from this page.1

A public group has four different forms: 1

  1. Public all (open) — This public group can be seen and joined by all TI members. This group is listed in the Group Directory, accessible by all.
  2. Public all (moderated) — All TI members see this public group and all TI members can request to belong to this group. However, a moderator of the group must approve their membership before they can become a member of the group. This group is listed in the Group Directory, accessible by all.
  3. Public tenant only (open) — This public group is seen only by tenant members; only tenant members can join this groups and see it listed in the Group Directory.
  4. Public tenant only (moderated) — This public group is seen only by tenant members; only tenant members can request to join this groups and see it listed in the Group Directory. The tenant member must be approved by the group moderator before they can become a member of the group.

5.2 Tenants

Tenant groups are made up of subgroups. The subgroups function the same as the public and private groups described above. (Note: Check to see how (or if) Prometheus will work with the concept of subgroups. Discuss best options once this is understood from the software.) The large, main tenant group that encompasses the subgroups has additional functionality that the tenant has paid for:

  • Additional admin capabilities and admin control
  • Ability to add sub groups
  • Can create more than the standard individual allowance (2 groups and more than one message boards) per group.
  • Pay for additional groups and message boards. Standard package is XX.
  • Tenant members see their specific tenant materials in the navigation bar with the Me and Community tabs.

The main tenant large group functions like a public moderated group. It can also be completely private if the tenant requests that functionality.

  • Classes and public groups are listed on the Tenant’s Group Page, viewable by members of the Tenant Group.
  • Tenants have a public page viewable by all TI members. (Unless the Tenant pays extra to be completely private.) Tenants can customize this page further for the public.
  • Tenant members become tenant members in two ways
    • the same as all private groups; initiators (in this case the Tenant administrator) sends an invitation through the system to the potential member. This invitation contains the Group ID and password.
    • A list of TI member and non TI members can be entered. When a member logs in or a new member signs up and logs in for the first time, they are automatically part of the tenant. They see the tenant listed under Me and it appears as a tab in their navigation.
  • Members of tenant groups who want to form a group within the tenant organization must ask the administrator of the larger tenant group to set up their group — public or private. These members are then given administrative privileges to administer that group.
  • Requests by members are sent via email through the system; on the main Tenant’s Group Page, there is an email link to the Tenant administrator and TI staff.

6.0 Gaining membership to Groups

6.1 Invitations 1

In both private and public groups, members of a group can send an invitation from the group inviting a TAPPED IN member or nonmember to join the group. The member sending the email must know the person’s member name or email address. Automatically included in the email is the URL for the group; and if it is a private group, the ID and password. The sender can include a text message as well. Since they are invited, a moderator does not need to approve their membership.

6.2 Open signup 1

Members of the community can sign up for the group at any time without a moderator’s approval

6.3 Moderator approved 1

Members of the community can sign up for the group at any time, but need a moderator’s approval to become a member. If the member has been invited by a moderator or the initator, there is no need for approval. Other group members can invite, but moderator approval needed for them to join.

The flow of moderator approval is as follows:

User logs in with their username, requesting to join this group.

Moderator is notified via email and in their office when they log in there is an announcement. The announcement is clickable.

The announcement links to Member Approval page

On Member Approval page, the administrator sees:

Member’s name

Link to Identity page

Any comments/reasons for joining that the member may have sent

The Moderator then chooses Approve or Reject. The Approval Message page confirms the selection and offers the opportunity to customize a message to the user:

+ Approve (Welcome message is generated)

+ Reject (Some reason for rejecting members must be written)
