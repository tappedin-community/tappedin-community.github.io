Student Accounts

Priorities for features indicated in brackets []. Priorities are marked with a 1 (must have); 2 (should have); or a 3 (may have). If a feature is marked but its subfeatures are not, this means that the subfeatures have the same priority as the larger feature.

Total days of work, estimated: 52 (drops to 32 days if we drop the filtering in chat requirement, which would prohibit interaction with students in SAC public areas anyway).

Student user information [mostly priority 1]
Estimated days: 2 (fairly straighforward)

Student accounts are special accounts that require sponsorship by a regular Tapped In Member [1]. The only required information for a student account is a username and password[1]. The real name and real email address for the student account is optional[1]. If not specified, the real name will be blank and a bogus value will be generated for the email address (Expresso requires that all accounts have email addresses)[1]. A member profile will not be kept for student accounts [1].

Members and the sponsor/moderators of a student group can see an info page for student accounts, accessible from the About link in the student group room. The student info page will state that the account is a student account, and show the student's username, the group they are a member of, the owner/sponsor of the group, and a real name of the student if the sponsor/moderators have entered one for the student, or the student has set his/her own real name[1].

Student accounts will not be listed in the Tapped In (or any tenant) directory[2]. Students also can not see the regular Tapped In (or any tenant) directory[2].

Student and Tapped In member groups, objects, and room access [mostly priority 1]
Estimated days: 10 (medium hard, lots of details, touches lots of code, lots to test/debug)

Like guest accounts, student accounts can not create groups or have offices[1], and they cannot set their home room[1]. By default, they can create items and upload files to their group room[1]. As in regular group rooms, student group owners/moderators can change individual student member permissions to disallow them adding items, etc.[1].

Student accounts are associated with exactly one (and only one) group room in the Student Activity Center[1]. Student group rooms are always private[1] and reside on the 2nd floor of the SAC[1]. Student groups, being private, do not appear in any group directories[1]; however, they do appear in a Groups listing in the SAC reception[1], even though they are private. Student group rooms always have "(K-12)" automatically appended to the end in any web display.

The owner/sponsor of the student group can also invite regular Tapped In members to join the student group, and can make other regular members moderators of the student group[1]. Tapped In members can not apply to join student groups however; they can only join a student group if they are invited[1].

By default, students can enter their group room at any time, but the sponsor/moderators of the student group can change permissions so that their students can only enter their group room at certain times, like when the sponsor is logged in [3].

Be default, students can only enter their group room in the SAC[1] and the public rooms (Reception, conference rooms) in the SAC building. Students can never go outside of the Student Activity Center (SAC) building [1]. The sponsor/moderators of a student group can turn off the ability of individual students or their entire student group to access public SAC rooms [3].

Regular Tapped In members can enter the SAC Reception area and the SAC public conference rooms [1]. Guests can not enter the SAC [1].

K-12 Students Online Discussion Group for Tapped In Members[priority 2]
Estimated days: 1 (fairly straightforward, need content)

A regular Tapped In group named "K-12 students online discussion group" will be created for teachers who manage student accounts. so that teachers with student accounts could talk to each other to exchange ideas, and teachers who are thinking of doing this can learn about it from others who have done this[1].

The group will be seeded with links and notes about activities previous (old) Tapped In members have done with students online, reflection on what was successful, and ideas to try[2]. It will also contain links to the terms of bringing K-12 students online[2].

During the process of creating a Tapped In student group, users are asked if they want to join this group[2]. If they choose to join, they will be automatically added as a member[2].

The "Create a K-12 student group" option only appears under the Tapped In Groups directory; it does not appear under any tenant group directories because student groups are never associated with tenants[1].

They are told that from the group room, they can optionally configure student accounts by assigning real names and unique passwords to each account[2], and apply to extend the duration of the project from the default (6 months?)[3]

The sponsor will also be emailed a list of account names and passwords for their group, upon creation[1].

Modifying accounts [mostly priority 3, some 2]
Estimated days: 5 (fairtly straighforward except for expiration, and how to delete vs. archive?)

In the student group room, the sponsor/moderators can edit student accounts under a new Edit Student Accounts option under the Settings menu.[2] (Note: consider merging this with the existing Edit membership option?) An edit link appears beside each student username; the sponsor/moderators can click on an edit link to assign a real name to a student account or change the account email address or password[2]. On the page where a teacher submits updated student information, there is also a checkbox asking if they want the new information to be mailed to the student.[3].

Sponsors can also turn on or off transcripts for the student [3]. (Note, the ability to turn on or off transcripts is a general priority 3 Tapped In feature.)

By default, students can not edit their own information, including their real name or email address. They have no Settings subtab under the Me tab[1]. Sponsors/moderators can turn on the ability for students to edit their own information[3].

From the group room Settings menu, the sponsor (only; not the moderators) can also change the expiration date for up to a year later[3]. Two weeks before a student group will be expiring, the sponsor will be sent an email telling them that their group and accounts will go away if they do not extend the duration in the group settings area[3]. If they do not extend, they will be emailed a final reminder 2 days before the group will be deleted (if not renewed)[3].
