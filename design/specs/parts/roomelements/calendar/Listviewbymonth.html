* Paarts: FluidArts
4:00-5:00pm PDT/7:00-8:00pm PDT
Join Deborah Phelan of FluidArts to discuss the significant role the arts can play in developing culturally determined media literacy and cross-cultural communication. FluidArts, a nonprofit organization, was founded by Millenaire, Leonardo and Miksike two years ago. Through extensive virtual cooperation, the organization is about to launch its first international project, WaterRoots. This multilingual project, housed on Miksike's Student Factory in Estonia, focuses initially on Migration and the role Water plays in the artistic and everyday life of diverse cultures. WaterRoots is the educational component of Leonardo's three-year international project, "The Spirit and Power of Water." Ms. Phelan and other representatives from FluidArts will be on hand to discuss the work they have done towards creating a 24/7 multilingual student factory and the groundwork they have laid towards the establishment of a multilingual, multi-literate cache of online mentors - SAEs, university students, artists, scientists and educators. Join this discussion on December 3.

Social Studies Teachers

* Social Studies Forum: Young Historians Project
5:00pm-6:00pm PDT/8:00pm-9:00pm EDT
Michael Hutchinson, discussion group leader, invites you to join the Social Studies discussion group to share resources. The Young Historians Project (YHP) is an educational multimedia initiative conducted by MIT and Harvard students in response to the 9/11/01 attacks. The purpose of YHP is to turn the tragedy of 9/11/01 into an educational and expressive opportunity for adolescents. YHP will invite junior and high school students to write their own history of 9/11/01 from any perspective and in any medium they choose. Their works will then be arranged into an online exhibit with some being chosen for display in the museums of the Boston, New York City, and Washington D.C. Historical Societies providing students with access to the works and viewpoints of their peers around the country. Participants in the discussion will include YHP Project Director, Dr. Shigeru Miyagawa of MIT's Foreign Languages and Literatures, and Comparative Media Studies departments, project team member Kimberly Zern, Master's student at Harvard Graduate School of Education, and several graduate and undergraduate student researchers from MIT. Join in for this discussion on December 12.
