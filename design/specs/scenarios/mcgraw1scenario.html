Tammy McGraw Scenario 1: Amy (current)

Amy, a recently retired teacher, was selected to serve as an Exemplary Educator (EE) in Tennessee for 2 years. She is one of 41 selected to provide additional support to the schools in greatest need of improved student achievement. The primary focus is on improved teaching and learning. Amy is familiar with email and has participated in listservs in the past. She is comfortable with Web searches and has done several in the past few years as she identified resources for her lessons.

Background: Main character, Amy, her occupation and level of familiarity with technology

Context: Exemplary Educators program

Time period: 1 year

As an EE, Amy received a great deal of face-to-face training with a consultant from AEL, one of the ten regional educational laboratories. She participated in a weeklong retreat at the beginning of her term where she met other EEs and participated in community-building activities. One session at the retreat focused on using technology to sustain the community of EEs. A listserv was created (hosted by AEL), and Amy received training on the use of listservs. Because she was familiar with them, she has no problems using it. It has allowed for a greater sense of community and has increased communication among the EEs. In the beginning, the discussions were seeded by the consultant at AEL. However, within a few short weeks EEs began and sustained their own conversations about problems, concerns, successes, and lessons learned.

Activities: asynchronous discussions, community participation

Once the work began in the schools, the consultant introduced a Web-based journaling component. Amy received instruction before she was expected to use the technology. She enters a password-protected site, selects her name from a drop-down menu, and opens her personal journal. There, she keys in what she is doing in her two schools. She makes daily or weekly entries, depending on how much time she has available. A template guides her to address specific details, including her activities, the people she is working with, and information about what is working and what isn’t working. The research staff at AEL uses this data to assist in the program evaluation.

Activities: Web-based journaling, community participation, capturing procedural data for evaluative purposes

Amy has positioned herself to be a leader in the EE program. She makes herself available for phone calls, emails, listserv discussions, and face-to-face meetings with other EEs as well as consultants. She shares her experiences and mentors new EEs at they enter the program.

Objective: build community, increase communication, share resources
