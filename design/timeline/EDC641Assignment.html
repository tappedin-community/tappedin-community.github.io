PROTOTYPING IDEAS

November - Mark Schlager, Patricia Schank, & Richard Godard deliver their white paper called "TAPPED IN to Teacher Professional Development: An On-line Conference Center for Education Communities"at MUD conference in Boston. In their presentation, they announce the birth of the "Teacher Professional Development Institute." (TAPPED IN)

 


CREATING COMMUNITY

February: TI begins collecting user data.

July/August: Two 2-week Tapped-In Summer Institutes held.

October: Project staff held initial real-time meetings in TI.

November: "After School Online" (ASO) begins

December: Official Start Date for National Science Foundation REC grant ($1,123,336 – P.I. Mark Schlager)

 BUILDING AWARENESS

January: Project staff held set of real-time meetings in TI.

March: Established MeetMe@tappedin.sri.com mailing list.

May: Project staff held set of real-time meetings in TI.

August: "The New York Times" features TI in an article under the headline: "How to Get the Most From Computers in the Classroom"

November: "The Technology Source" names TI its Spotlight Site of the Month.

 GROWING MEMBERSHIP

May: "Edutopia" features TI in an article under the headline "TAPPED IN: Connecting Communities of Learners Online"

July: First annual Summer Carnival Day at TI.

October: TI has 15 tenant organizations and 5800 members.

.		 DEMONSTRATING VALUE

July: Second annual Summer Carnival Day at TI

September: Chris Dede recognizes TI in his white paper titled "The Role Of Emerging Technologies For Knowledge Mobilization, Dissemination, and Use in Education." The work was commissioned by the Office of Educational Research and Improvement, U.S. Department of Education. Margaret Riel recognizes TI in her white paper titled "New Designs for Connected Teaching and Learning" which she delivered at the U. S. Department of Education Secretary's Conference on Educational Technology.

October: "The Chronicle of Higher Education" features TI in an article under the headline "Instructors Try Out Updated MOOs as Online-Course Classrooms."

 SUSTAINING LEADERSHIP

April: "Dansk Paedagogisk Tidsskrift," a peer-reviewed journal of education published in Denmark, includes a review of TI, bringing it international attention.

August: Summer Carnival moves to August for its theme of Crazy Eights.

November: NSF REC grant expires.

 MAINTAINING ROUTINES

July: Fourth annual Summer Carnival Day at TI. Mark S. Schlager, Judith Fusco, and Patricia Schank co-author a chapter published in “Building virtual communities: Learning and change in cyberspace.” NY: Cambridge University Press

November: Patricia Schank, Alexandra Harris, and Mark Schlager deliver their white paper “Painting a Landscape onto TAPPED IN 2” at the MUD conference in New Orleans, LA. In their presentation, they announce the impending birth of TI2.

 EVOLVING INFRASTRUCTURE

March: "The Philadelphia Inquirer" features TI in its tech.life@school column.

June: TI has 17,000 members.

July: Fifth annual Summer Carnival Day celebrates debut of Tapped In 2 by changing its name to “Tapped In Festival.” Tapped In 2 debuts.

November: "Harvard Education Letter" includes TI in an article under the headline "Linking Teachers with Technology."

 FORGING AHEAD

February: "Learning and Leading with Technology" features TI in its "Mining the Internet" feature story.

July: Sixth Summer event, now called Tapped In Festival.
